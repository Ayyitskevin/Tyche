# Tyche — "Bloomberg for the Masses" Gap Analysis & Roadmap

> Generated from a multi-source research pass (11-agent workflow: codebase inventory + Gödel / Bloomberg-taxonomy / retail-alternative / equity-depth / cross-asset / news-AI / data-source research → gap synthesis → completeness critic → reconciliation). 102 sources collected. Every recommendation is filtered through Tyche's invariants: research-only, no data resale (keyless-public or BYO-key only), provenance-stamped, keyless-mock, clean-room.

## Executive summary

Tyche already matches the retail-terminal command surface and genuinely leads Gödel on keyless crypto depth (BOOK/FUND/DEX), a public REST/SSE API, fully-offline mock mode, per-datum provenance stamping, and a grounded no-advice AI copilot — but most of its equity surface (prices, estimates, ratings, options, news) is mock, so it currently reads as a demo rather than a terminal. The highest value-per-effort is analytics Tyche can add with ZERO new data: full statement depth + ratios/common-size/TTM, a real technical-indicator library, portfolio risk (beta/correlation/VaR — its single highest-leverage build, now P0), options analytics, DCF, ECO macro transforms, and backtesting, since it already fetches every input (SEC XBRL company-facts, OHLCV candles, option-chain structures, FRED series, positions). A SEC-EDGAR harvest is the biggest keyless data win — flipping 13F holdings, insider Form 4, 13D/13G activist stakes, full-text search/alerts, short interest, and a cross-sectional screener from mock to license-clean-real — and a Treasury/FRED yield curve plus FINRA-TRACE single-name bond prices close most of the wholly-unwired fixed-income domain keylessly (correcting the draft's over-block of corporate bonds). The unavoidable gaps — real listed-equity/options feeds, consensus estimates, and especially earnings-call transcripts — are licensed by nature and must ship as BYO-key adapters, never bundled; note too that valuation analytics (RV/DCF/quality score) are only "real" in BYO-key price mode, and a few FRED-hosted third-party series (ICE BofA OAS, S&P) are display-only BYO and must be excluded from bundled exports. Net: sequence analytics-only and keyless-public wins first, wire a BYO-key LLM to activate the already-built copilot (contained M-effort slot-in), and state clearly that order routing (BROK), messaging (CHAT/MSG), and branded ESG/broker research are out of scope or permanently BYO-key.

## Positioning

Tyche is "Bloomberg for the masses" not by out-buying the incumbents on data — it will never bundle or resell a licensed equity, options, estimates, or transcript feed — but by being the open, provenance-stamped, research-only terminal that is fully usable offline for free and real wherever public data allows. Its honest edge is threefold: (1) the deepest keyless stack anyone ships for the price — SEC EDGAR fundamentals + ownership (13F, insider, 13D/13G) + filings + full-text + short interest, US Treasury/FRED rates, FINRA-TRACE bond prices, GDELT news, and genuinely deep crypto microstructure — and a sharper honesty than most critics grant: a surprising amount of "equity data" is actually keyless, while the flip side is that a few FRED-hosted series (ICE BofA OAS, S&P) are third-party copyright and can only be displayed under the user's own key, never re-exported; (2) analytics depth that costs almost nothing to add because the inputs are already in hand (ratios, TA library, portfolio risk, ECO macro transforms, options and DCF math), with the caveat that price-based valuation outputs are real only in BYO-key price mode; and (3) a grounded, cited, no-advice AI copilot plus a documented public API that the paywalled incumbents simply do not offer. The pitch: every category on Bloomberg's public function taxonomy, demoable offline at zero cost, real where public data reaches, BYO-key where it doesn't — with every datum and export stamped by source, mode, and freshness, and licensed third-party series fenced out of anything Tyche redistributes.

## The shape of the gap

**55 gaps** identified, and the encouraging headline is how few need licensed data:

| Data source kind | Count | Meaning |
|---|---:|---|
| Analytics only (no new data) | 22 | Compute over inputs Tyche **already fetches** — zero new data, zero licensing. |
| Keyless-public data | 17 | A free/keyless public API Tyche can bundle (SEC EDGAR, Treasury, FRED, GDELT, FINRA, …). |
| BYO-key data | 13 | Needs the **user's own** API key/entitlement — ship the adapter, never bundle the feed. |
| Licensed / blocked | 3 | Proprietary/licensed or invariant-blocked — BYO-key only or out of scope. |

**39 of 55 (71%) need no licensed data at all.** That is the whole thesis: Tyche can close most of the distance to a Bloomberg-class terminal without ever buying or reselling a feed.

## Cross-cutting themes

1. The biggest, fastest wins need NO new data: statement depth + ratios/common-size/YoY/TTM, the full TA indicator library, portfolio risk (beta/correlation/VaR/Sortino) — promoted to P0 as the single highest-leverage build — the options analytics suite, DCF, ECO macro transforms, and backtesting are all pure analytics over inputs Tyche already fetches (SEC XBRL company-facts, OHLCV candles, option chains, FRED series, positions): zero data, zero licensing, zero invariant risk.
2. SEC EDGAR is a massively under-exploited keyless asset: the same in-tree adapter can power real 13F institutional holdings, insider Form 3/4/5, 13D/13G activist stakes, filing full-text search (EFTS), filing-triggered alerts, a whole-market fundamentals screener (Financial Statement Data Sets), and segment revenue — all license-clean and bundleable, none blocked by the no-resale invariant.
3. The draft under-counted keyless equity data: short interest (FINRA/exchanges), single-name corporate/agency bond prices (FINRA TRACE), and 13D/13G stakes are all keyless — not the licensed feeds the draft implicitly assumed all equity/FI data to be. Real-time listed-equity/options quotes remain the genuinely licensed core and stay BYO-key.
4. ECO/macro is an under-leveraged real-data strength: Tyche ships real FRED series but only line+table depth, and has no economic release calendar at all. Adding YoY/QoQ transforms, multi-series overlay, correlation, and recession shading is analytics-only, and a keyless release calendar (BLS/BEA/Census schedules + FRED actuals, consensus overlay BYO-key) is a Bloomberg ECO table stake — both were missing from the draft.
5. Valuation analytics silently inherit the equity-price gap: RV comps, DCF/reverse-DCF, and the composite quality score compute over MOCK prices by default and are 'real' only once a BYO-key equity feed is wired — market them as analytics wins but flag the price dependency rather than as fully keyless.
6. Earnings-call transcripts are the single biggest content gap AND genuinely licensed — no robust keyless source (only partial 8-K EX-99 prepared remarks, not Q&A), so BYO-key-only, never bundled. Say so honestly rather than roadmapping it as keyless.
7. Real listed-equity/options price data is the core credibility gap with NO keyless source (exchanges/OPRA license it): the whole DES/GP/GIP/HP/QM/W and OMON surface is mock today, so it must be a BYO-key adapter by nature — ship adapter + mock parity, never a bundled feed.
8. The AI copilot is architecturally ahead of the field on the right axis (already grounded, cited, provenance-stamped, no-advice-guarded, and fed recentCommands) but is a deterministic mock; wiring a BYO-key LLM + streaming is a contained M-effort slot-in — and the existing guardrail must wrap the real model, not just the mock. Copilot-driven navigation is a further research-safe workflow multiplier.
9. Fixed income is the one whole domain entirely unwired end-to-end (bonds/futures are registered capabilities but have no schema/method/mock); the keyless on-ramp — typed contracts + mock, Treasury/FRED yield curve, FINRA-TRACE single-name prices, and analytics-only bond math — closes the most conspicuous Bloomberg-taxonomy hole cheaply.
10. Licensing has a subtle export boundary the draft's own strength exposes: a few FRED-hosted series (ICE BofA OAS, S&P indices) are third-party copyright and must be display-only under the user's FRED key and excluded from provenance-stamped bundled exports, or the export feature itself leaks redistribution (invariant #2).
11. Fair-value / margin-of-safety and the 'value' axis of any composite score edge toward investment advice (invariant #1) if surfaced as a single undervalued/overvalued verdict — they must stay strictly scenario/percentile/educational, the riskiest framing to guard.
12. A handful of marquee categories are permanently out and should be stated as non-actionable, not roadmapped: order routing/brokerage execution (BROK) violates research-only invariant #1; messaging/chat (CHAT/MSG/IB) is a deliberate scope non-goal; and branded ESG scores, supply-chain graphs (SPLC), broker research (BI/RES), and full OPRA options flow are proprietary/licensed — BYO-key only, never bundled.
