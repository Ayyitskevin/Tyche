## Claim themes (per WebSearch snippets; low confidence)

- **Positioning as a Bloomberg alternative/"killer"** (videos 2, 3, 6, 9) — core narrative.
- **News / press-release speed** as a trading edge ("~30 seconds ahead") (video 4).
- **Browser-based CLI workflow / live demo** of commands (videos 1, 10).
- **Expert networks / large contacts database** (video 5).
- **AI Analyst on the roadmap** (video 7) — a *future* capability claim, not shipped.
- **Product roadmap discussion** (video 8).

## Product lessons for Tyche (from the *themes*, not the content)

1. The competitor's loudest message is **price + speed + Bloomberg-muscle-memory**. Tyche can't win
   on licensed-data speed, so it must win on **delivery model + transparency** (self-host, mock-first,
   provenance, open SDK).
2. **"AI Analyst" is a roadmap promise.** Tyche already ships a *grounded, no-advice* copilot — an
   opportunity to be **more rigorous** (citations, workspace-grounded) than a hype-led AI feature.
3. Demos lean on **single-name analyst flow** and **news**; Tyche's existing slice already covers the
   analyst flow — polish it and add news filters to be demo-credible.
