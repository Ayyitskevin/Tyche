FAQ

Where does the market data come from?

Seven real adapters ship today, most of them keyless: Binance (crypto depth/funding/candles, live), Dexscreener (on-chain DEX pools), Frankfurter/ECB (FX, EOD), Stooq (equities/ETFs/indices, EOD), and GDELT (global news) — no keys at all; SEC EDGAR (filings) needs only a contact email; FRED (macro) uses a free key. A deterministic mock provider covers everything else, so the terminal is fully usable with zero keys. Real-time equity quotes are a bring-your-own-key upgrade. We don't bundle or resell licensed market data — exchange licensing makes "cheap terminal with real-time everything" a lie somewhere; ours is priced for what it honestly is.

Is this investment advice? Can I trade through it?

No and no. Tyche is research software: it displays data and educational analytics, never buy/sell/hold recommendations, and contains no order-placement path. The AI copilot cites its sources and declines advice questions.

What happens to my data if I cancel?

Nothing is deleted when a trial or subscription lapses — you hit a paywall, your data waits, and the Export my data button keeps working from the paywall itself: your whole account (workspaces, watchlists, notes, alerts, portfolios, preferences) downloads as one JSON file. Table views also export CSV. The self-host edition runs the same code, so you can leave with everything — and a password-confirmed account deletion is one click away if you want us to keep nothing.

How is hosted different from self-host?

Same terminal. Hosted adds accounts with hard per-account data isolation, TLS, backups, upgrades, and sync across your devices — the boring ops you'd otherwise run yourself.

Is it really keyboard-first?

⌘K focuses the command bar; every command has autocomplete with fuzzy matching; Tab cycles panels; chords are rebindable. You can use the mouse, but you won't need to.
